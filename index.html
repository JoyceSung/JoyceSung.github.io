About

Researcher with 5 Years of Expertise in Cancer Biology and Computational Biology


I hold a master's degree in Biomedical Electronics and Bioinformatics from National Taiwan University (NTU) and earned a bachelor's degree in Life Science from National Tsing Hua University (NTHU). I currently serve as a research assistant at the Graduate Institute of Biomedical Electronics and Bioinformatics at NTU.


With five years of dedicated research experience, my expertise lies in the domains of cancer biology, genomics, bioinformatics, and computational biology. Currently, I am actively engaged in the development of a pioneering computational approach aimed at expanding our understanding of cancer variations and immunotherapy. My primary research objective is to establish a comprehensive pipeline and innovative tools for the identification of diverse neoantigens using multi-omics data. This research is now submitted for publication and a patent application, making it publicly accessible and poised to make a significant contribution to the academic field.


My profound interest in cancer biology was initially ignited during my undergraduate project, which focused on the early diagnosis of cancer. My extensive research experience, combined with my interdisciplinary background, has provided me with valuable insights into the fields of cancer biology, computational biology, and molecular biology. I specialize in multi-omics data analysis, computational tool development, genomic data analysis, machine learning, and more.
